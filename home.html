PAGABLAN

Threaded into Ilocano heritage, the pagablan of Pinili preserves the art of weaving. A symbol of skill and tradition, it continues to weave stories of resilience and craftsmanship. "Inabel" comes from the Ilocano word "abel," which means to weave. This age-old tradition of handweaving dates back to pre-colonial times, when textiles were essential to the everyday lives of Filipinos. In the Ilocos region, weaving was a common household activity, and textiles were traded with neighboring communities for goods such as food and tools.
